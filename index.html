Introducing myself..

Hi everyone! My name is Irida. I have been teaching greek to chinese students, who live or not in Greece and attend or not greek schools, for many years now.

Check more in My Linkedin Profile.

How did this journey begin..

Everything started many years ago, when I met my first chinese student. Of course at first she was just a little girl I met somewhere, who caught my eye because of her adorable personality. Her family got to know me better and finally asked me if I can teach her greek. When I decided to teach for the first time, someone who was foreign speaker I didn't have any official material to work with. I was working only with a book I had found online (made by volunteer teachers) and whatever material I created by myself (exercises, texts etc). Despite the difficulties, I managed to teach that little girl greek, she managed to be part of my society and one led to another and I had the blessing to create a very big circle of chinese students. Of course, after all these years, the material I use has changed, became better, more effecient and more qualified for this difficult but intersting journey of knowledge. After all these years, I grow more and more love for this job and of course my students. I also felt the need and interest to learn chinese and let this fact bring me closer to my students' customs and culture. My goal is to be able to share this ancient and beautiful language with even more students with bright minds and real thirst to learn new cultures and languages.

How do I start the courses?

First of all, there are many levels in greek language. If you already have been taught greek with or without teacher in the past, we will check your level of knowledge and we will place on the right following level. If not, depending on your age, we will start from the beginning using the suitable material.

What parts of learning does the material contain and exercise?

  1. Reading
  2. Comprehension of reading and answering questions
  3. Vocabulary
  4. Grammar
  5. Listening
  6. Speaking

How many levels of greek language are there?

  • A1
  • A2
  • B1
  • B2
  • Γ1 (C1)
  • Γ2 (C2)
